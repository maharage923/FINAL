Space is big scary

I know, right?

Posted by Ooga Booga at High Noon

Have you ever wondered why, in Christmas movies that later show the real Santa, none of the parents or nonbelievers talk about where they think the presents come from? Santa usually says that everybody gets presents and is either naughy or nice, but when presents show up on Christmas morning, like in the Polar Express, why don't the parents ask the kids, "no, who is that really from? Who is Mr. C, one of your teachers?" I've thought extensively about this and as formulaic as many Christmas movies are, they always neglect to explain this little plothole. These are the kinds of things I wonder about during the day.

Here's another recent movie complaint. In Black Panther: Wakanda Forever, why do the Talokanians keep going after that annoying American girl in the knockoff Iron Man suit without saying "hey, she's just some kid from the hood, are we sure our intel is correct?" or "Even if she helped build or program those vibranium-stealing boats, do we really think she's out to get us or even cares about our vibranium enough for us to justify attacking a huge, technologically-advanced country for no reason?" Much of that battle could have been avoided and they could have spent more time talking about the much more interesting conflicts surrounding vibranium mining between these two secret nations. Just saying.

How did Maz Kanata get Anakin's lightsaber after Luke lost it with his hand in Cloud City? And why are the new Star Wars movies rubbish?

Why isn't there one universal Sign Language?
